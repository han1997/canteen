# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Primary users are police officers ordering breakfast, lunch, or dinner from a WeChat mini-program inside an internal network context. Their main job is to check available meal slots, choose dishes or packages quickly, adjust quantities, and submit or cancel orders before the cutoff.

Operational users include kitchen staff who verify orders, manage meal availability, review daily totals, and export meal data. Admin and super admin users manage people, roles, meal templates, ordering windows, bulk imports, statistics, and audit-sensitive operations.

Use happens on mobile screens, often between other work. The interface should support fast scanning, confident action, and clear status recognition without requiring explanation.

## Product Purpose

公安食堂订餐 is an internal canteen ordering system for coordinating daily meal demand across officers, kitchen staff, and administrators. It exists to make meal ordering, cutoff control, kitchen preparation, statistics, and exports reliable in a controlled intranet environment.

Success means officers can place the correct order with minimal friction, kitchen staff can see accurate demand before service, and administrators can maintain users, dishes, permissions, and reports without ambiguity.

## Brand Personality

The product should feel 稳重、清晰、高效. The tone is practical, direct, and trustworthy, with enough warmth for a daily meal service but no decorative cheerfulness.

Copy should be concise and operational. Labels should favor familiar Chinese administrative language where it improves clarity, but avoid stiff legacy wording when a shorter action label works better.

## Anti-references

Do not make the product feel like an internet marketing site, a playful consumer app, or an outdated government system. Avoid exaggerated hero treatment, promotional copy, loud gradients, overly cute icons, novelty interactions, and dense legacy form screens with weak hierarchy.

The interface should not look celebratory, childish, sales-driven, or bureaucratically stale. It should feel like a dependable internal tool that people can trust immediately.

## Design Principles

1. Prioritize the task path. Ordering, managing slots, checking totals, and exporting data should be reachable through clear, predictable flows.
2. Make status unmistakable. Open, closed, selected, submitted, verified, cancelled, expired, loading, and error states need consistent visual treatment and plain language.
3. Respect operational density. Admin and kitchen views can carry many numbers and controls, but the structure must make scanning faster rather than heavier.
4. Keep trust visible. Role boundaries, audit-sensitive actions, destructive changes, and export tasks should communicate authority and consequence without alarmist styling.
5. Use restraint with warmth. The interface may feel approachable because it is about meals, but the primary impression should remain stable, orderly, and efficient.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No explicit accessibility requirement has been set. Still preserve baseline usability: readable Chinese text on small phone screens, sufficient contrast for status and action states, touch targets sized for mobile use, and meaning that does not rely on color alone.
